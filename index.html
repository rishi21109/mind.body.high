Developing Healthy Habits in High School and College

The habits we inculcate in high school and in college are ones that we apply during the rest of our lives...

Guidelines from the National Institute of Mental Health:

  • Regular exercise: Physical and mental health are directly linked.
  • Eat regular and healthy meals: A balanced diet can help boost focus.
  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep: Being well-rested boosts productivity and mood.
  • Try some relaxing activity: This can be a sport or something like reading.
  • Set goals and priorities: Breaking down your tasks into manageable pieces can reduce stress.
  • Practice gratitude: It is often easy to slip into sadness and forget how much we have.

Academic Pressure Among High School Students

As per a study in the Indian Journal of Applied Science, children who go to private schools face far more stress...

Findings from the study:

  • More homework and increased parental pressure.
  • Despondency and emotional support are negatively correlated.
  • Most school students do not receive proper emotional guidance.

Rising Student Suicide Rates in India

In India, student suicide rates have increased by 4% in 2024 compared to 2023...

Key issues identified:

  • Many officials suspect that student suicides are underreported.
  • The "coaching center" dynamic remains unregulated.
  • Increased academic pressure due to lack of formal supervision.

Physical Health and Nutrition in High School

It is also extremely necessary that students maintain a healthy physical state...

CDC Report Findings:

  • Only 41.8% of students eat fresh fruits regularly.
  • Less than half of students engage in 60 minutes of daily physical activity.
  • 16% of students skip breakfast entirely over the week.
